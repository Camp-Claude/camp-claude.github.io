# Claude Foundations — A 4-Session Curriculum for ChatGPT Migrants

*A short virtual cohort that starts where most paying learners actually are, and funnels into the full Camp Claude.*

**Working draft — April 17, 2026**

---

## Why This Curriculum Exists

The full Camp Claude cohort is designed for true beginners — learners who may not know what an LLM is. That audience exists, and the full cohort is the right product for them.

But most people who will pay for Claude training are already *using AI* — typically ChatGPT, sometimes Gemini or Copilot. They don't need the LLM ground floor. They need to understand what's *different* about Claude and why they'd switch or add it.

Claude Foundations is the product for that audience. Four one-hour virtual sessions, delivered weekly. Assumes ChatGPT baseline. Ends with a taste of Cowork and a clear path into the full cohort.

---

## The One-Sentence Version

**Claude Foundations teaches the three doors (Chat / Cowork / Code), the three levers (Skills / Connectors / Plugins), and ends with a taste of the automation payoff — enough to switch your default, not enough to build the full workflow.**

The full workflow is what the Camp Claude cohort is for. Foundations is the on-ramp.

---

## Audience

**Primary:** ChatGPT users who are curious about Claude but haven't made the switch, don't know what's different, or feel stuck using AI only for writing.

**Secondary:** Individual professionals and small-business owners who want AI to do more than draft text.

**Explicitly not for:**
- True beginners who haven't used any AI assistant yet → route to the full Camp Claude cohort, which starts from zero.
- Developers → route to Code-focused training.

---

## Shape

- **Format:** Virtual (Zoom or equivalent). Not in-person.
- **Duration:** 4 sessions × 60 minutes.
- **Cadence:** Weekly (4 weeks total).
- **Cohort size:** 20–30 learners. Screen-share and breakout rooms make this doable solo.
- **Pre-work:** Lightweight — the Welcome Packet from Camp Claude, plus a 2-minute "install the desktop app" guide.
- **Community:** Shared Circle or Skool space, same as full cohort (Foundations grads inherit it).
- **Price range:** $149–$399 per seat, tiered early-bird / standard / late.

## Session 1 — Why Claude (The Three Doors)

**Purpose.** Reset the learner's expectation of what Claude *is*. Most ChatGPT users assume Claude is "another chatbot." Session 1 shows them it's three surfaces, only one of which is the chatbot they already know.

**Learning objectives.**
- Identify the three doors (Chat, Cowork, Code) and what each is for.
- Install the Claude desktop app and log in.
- Articulate, in their own words, why Claude has three surfaces when ChatGPT has one.

**Core content.**
- The three-tab tour (*see `interface-tour.md`*).
- Chat vs Cowork vs Code (*see `chat-vs-cowork-vs-code.md`*).
- Why ChatGPT's Canvas is *not* the same as Cowork — the sandbox is the point.
- Where Code fits, why most learners won't need it (the honest signpost).

**Exercise.** Install the desktop app together. Open each of the three tabs in turn. Notice the sidebar changes. Name out loud what each surface is for.

**Graduation moment.** The learner has the desktop app installed and can say, in plain English, which door they'd use for what.

**Assets used.**
- `interface-tour.md` (handout)
- `chat-vs-cowork-vs-code.md` (handout)

---

## Session 2 — Chat, Projects, Context, Artifacts

**Purpose.** Upgrade the learner's Chat skills from "I type things and Claude types back" to "I organize context and iterate toward the output I want."

**Learning objectives.**
- Use Chat Projects to scope context to a goal.
- Understand what a context window is and why Projects help manage it.
- Navigate Artifacts and use them as iterable documents rather than chat-buried text.
- Push back on Claude's output and get a better answer.

**Core content.**
- The Chat sidebar deep-dive (New chat, Projects, Customize, Artifacts).
- Chat Projects — what they are, what belongs in them (pinned files, custom instructions).
- Artifacts — the Claude differentiator ChatGPT migrants notice immediately.
- Prompting Claude — what's different from ChatGPT (tone, nuance, longer context windows).

**Exercise.** Pick a real task (a trip plan, a client proposal, a piece of writing the learner is stuck on) and build a Chat Project around it. Pin files, add custom instructions, iterate in Artifacts.

**Graduation moment.** The learner has one live Chat Project with an artifact they'd actually use.

**Notes.**
- Name the distinction between Chat Projects and Cowork Projects out loud. They are *different objects*. Learners will be confused later if this isn't said early.
- Artifacts is the single clearest "Claude has something ChatGPT doesn't" moment. Spend time here.

---

## Session 3 — Customize (Skills, Connectors, Plugins)

**Purpose.** Teach the three levers for shaping Claude. This is the turning point — after Session 3, the learner isn't just *using* Claude, they're *configuring* Claude to match how they work.

**Learning objectives.**
- Explain the difference between a skill, a connector, and a plugin.
- Install one connector and one skill during the session.
- Recognize the Customize button in all three surfaces as the same button.

**Core content.**
- Skills vs Connectors vs Plugins (*see `skills-vs-connectors-vs-plugins.md`*).
- Walking through Customize in the UI.
- Installing a connector — the moment Claude goes from "general" to "yours."
- Writing a tiny skill — markdown, plain English, saved as `CLAUDE.md`.

**Exercise.** Every learner installs a Gmail or Calendar connector live during the session, then writes a 10-line skill for a recurring task they care about. Share one out loud.

**Graduation moment.** The learner has at least one connector and one skill installed on their account, and has used both in a real Chat conversation.

**Assets used.**
- `skills-vs-connectors-vs-plugins.md` (handout)

**Notes.**
- Preview the automation equation at the end of the session: *"You now have two of the three ingredients. Next week we'll put them together."* This plants the hook for Session 4.

---

## Session 4 — A Taste of Cowork

**Purpose.** Show learners what Claude can actually *do* when the sandbox is on. This is where ChatGPT breaks down and Claude becomes a different kind of tool. Ends with the automation equation as the bridge to the full cohort.

**Learning objectives.**
- Open Cowork and run a real task on a real folder.
- Identify the three panels of an active task (Progress, Project, Context) and what each tells them.
- Understand what Scheduled tasks unlock, even if they don't build one today.
- Know what comes next — full Camp Claude cohort.

**Core content.**
- Creating a "Claude Test" folder on the desktop (the 90-second exercise from the syllabus).
- The three Cowork panels (*see `cowork-task-anatomy.md`*).
- Running one real task in Cowork — watching Claude read, plan, and act within the sandbox.
- The automation equation as preview: *Skill × Connector × Scheduled = Automation.* (*see `automation-equation.md`*)
- Where Camp Claude goes from here — the full cohort, the graduation automation, the Code track for the rare power user.

**Exercise.** Create the Claude Test folder. Put 2–3 files in it. Open Cowork on that folder. Give Claude a simple real task (e.g., "summarize these files in one paragraph each"). Review the Progress panel. Watch the Scratchpad update. Accept or reject.

**Graduation moment.** The learner has run one Cowork task successfully, read the three panels, and understands what Scheduled tasks do — even though they haven't built one.

**Assets used.**
- `cowork-task-anatomy.md` (handout)
- `automation-equation.md` (preview handout — full version used in the main cohort)

**Notes.**
- Close with the product ladder: *"You now know enough to switch your default AI to Claude. You don't yet know enough to build a full automation — that's the Camp Claude cohort. And if you ever want to code with Claude, that's a different track."*

## What's Not in Scope

Foundations is deliberately narrow. These things belong to the full cohort, not here:

- **Module 9 depth** (the sandbox exercise at length) — referenced, not taught in full.
- **Module 10** (Supervision and the Competency Ladder) — touched, not depth.
- **Module 11** (Workflow Automation — actually building one) — *previewed*, not built. This is the hook that funnels into the full cohort.
- **Code** — one slide of orientation in Session 1, then never again.
- **Dispatch** — not touched. Power-user feature.

Trying to cover these in 4 hours would feel rushed and leave the learner frustrated. Leaving them out creates a clear next step — which is the point.

---

## Graduation State

A learner who completes Foundations can:
- Switch their default AI from ChatGPT to Claude without feeling lost.
- Use Chat Projects to organize ongoing work.
- Iterate on Artifacts instead of copy-pasting from chat.
- Install and use at least one connector.
- Install and use at least one skill.
- Open Cowork, run a single task, and read the three panels.
- Articulate what Scheduled tasks do and why they're the next step.

A learner who completes Foundations *cannot* yet:
- Build a full automation end-to-end (this is the Camp Claude cohort).
- Supervise a complex multi-file task with confidence (also the cohort).
- Use Code (different track entirely).

That gap is intentional. Foundations leaves the learner *curious*, not *complete*. Curious is what funnels.

## Delivery Notes (Facilitator)

**Session shape (60 min).**
- 5 min — recap last week, acknowledgment of attendance
- 10 min — concept teaching (slides + narration)
- 20 min — live UI walkthrough / demo
- 15 min — learner exercise (with breakout rooms if needed)
- 10 min — share-out + Q&A + preview next session

**What to watch for.**
- ChatGPT muscle memory — learners will type ChatGPT-style prompts. Gently reframe: "Claude is happy with more context."
- Tab confusion — they'll forget which door they're in. Pause and re-orient.
- Customize button confusion — they'll try to install a connector three times, once per surface. Say clearly: *"It's the same button. Install once."*
- Sandbox surprise — in Session 4, learners will try to ask Cowork about files outside the folder. Let them. The surprise *is* the lesson.

**Co-facilitation.** Solo is doable up to ~25 learners if you're willing to monitor chat between teaching beats. Past 25, bring a co-facilitator to handle chat and one-on-one help in breakout rooms.

## Pricing & Positioning

**Standalone:** $149–$399 per seat, tiered early-bird / standard / late.

**Bundled with full cohort:** Offer Foundations as a free bonus or $99 deposit applied toward the full Camp Claude cohort price. This is probably the strongest positioning — Foundations *is* the lead funnel.

## The Product Ladder

Foundations sits at the bottom of a three-rung ladder. Every rung funnels into the next.

| Rung | Product | Audience | Duration | Price |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Claude Foundations | ChatGPT migrants | 4 × 60 min virtual | $149–$399 |
| **2** | Camp Claude (full cohort) | Non-technical users doing real work | 5 × 90 min + async | $499–$1,499 |
| **3** | Claude Code for Builders *(future)* | Technical users / power users | TBD | $1,500+ |

Foundations exists to fill rung 2. Rung 3 exists for the 5% of rung 2 grads who want the seatbelts off.
