# Camp Claude — Asset Library

*A complete directory of every asset needed to run Camp Claude as a cohort-based program with async pre-work, live sessions, and an ongoing community.*

**Working draft — April 10, 2026**

---

## Program Shape at a Glance

Camp Claude is a cohort-based program delivered in three layers:

1. **Pre-Camp (async)** — 4 short videos and a welcome packet that learners watch and read before the cohort begins.
2. **Live Cohort (synchronous)** — 5 live sessions, 90 minutes each, delivered online.
3. **Community (ongoing)** — a Circle or Skool space where cohorts interact before, during, and after camp, and where alumni stay connected.

The asset library is organized by layer, with a marketing and operations layer on top and an optional scaling layer on the bottom.

---

## 1. Marketing & Enrollment Assets

Everything a prospective learner sees before they say yes.

### 1.1 Landing Page
**Format:** Single web page (hosted).
**Description:** The public face of Camp Claude. Hero, promise, who it's for, what they'll build, curriculum at a glance, pricing, FAQ, enrollment CTA. The "become the person at your company who knows how to do this" pitch lives here.
**Build in:** Webflow, Framer, Carrd, or a simple custom site.

### 1.2 Sales Page (Long Form)
**Format:** Long-form web page.
**Description:** The deeper pitch for learners who need more convincing. Includes the problem, the transformation, testimonials, detailed curriculum, instructor bio, guarantee, and full FAQ.
**Build in:** Same platform as the landing page.

### 1.3 Enrollment Form
**Format:** Web form.
**Description:** How learners sign up. Captures name, email, role, company, cohort preference, and one qualifying question ("what's one task you want Claude to take off your plate?"). That answer becomes material for the first session.
**Build in:** Typeform, Tally, or a native Circle/Skool form.

### 1.4 Pricing & Tracks Page
**Format:** Web page section or standalone page.
**Description:** Clear breakdown of the two tracks — Open-Enrollment Cohort (individuals) and Private Company Cohort (teams). Explains what's included in each and how to book.
**Build in:** Same platform as the landing page.

### 1.5 Email Sequences
**Format:** Set of templated emails.
**Description:** Three sequences — (a) nurture sequence for interested-but-not-enrolled leads, (b) launch sequence for open enrollment periods, (c) post-enrollment welcome sequence that primes learners for the pre-work. Each is 3–7 emails.
**Build in:** ConvertKit, Mailchimp, Beehiiv, or similar.

### 1.6 Social Media Kit
**Format:** Folder of image templates and copy snippets.
**Description:** Reusable graphics and caption templates for announcing cohorts, sharing wins, and promoting alumni automations. Consistent visual identity.
**Build in:** Canva, Figma, or a PowerPoint template.

### 1.7 Case Studies
**Format:** 1–2 page Word docs or web pages.
**Description:** Alumni success stories. One case study per notable automation — what they built, how much time it saves, how it changed their work. Built after cohorts run, not before.
**Build in:** Word doc or web page, published as part of marketing.

## 2. Pre-Camp Assets (Async Pre-Work)

What every enrolled learner receives and completes before Session 1.

### 2.1 Video 1 — What Is This Thing?
**Format:** 5–7 minute video.
**Description:** Plain-language explainer of what an LLM and a chatbot actually are. No jargon. The "you are not talking to Google" video. Covers Module 0 from the syllabus.
**Build in:** Script in Word, slides in PowerPoint, recorded with screen capture plus talking head. Hosted on Vimeo or inside the community platform.

### 2.2 Video 2 — Chatbot vs. Agent
**Format:** 5–7 minute video.
**Description:** Side-by-side demo showing a chatbot guessing at "what's on my calendar tomorrow" vs. an agent actually checking. The lightbulb video. Covers Module 1.
**Build in:** Live screen recording of Claude performing both, with narration over the top.

### 2.3 Video 3 — The AI Landscape
**Format:** 5–8 minute video.
**Description:** Quick tour of Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and Perplexity. Feel comparison, not benchmark. Why someone would choose Claude for this training. Covers Module 2.
**Build in:** PowerPoint slides interleaved with short screen clips of each tool.

### 2.4 Video 4 — Ways to Access Claude
**Format:** 5–8 minute video.
**Description:** Tour of web, mobile, and desktop. Why the desktop app is the doorway. Ends with a direct instruction to install the desktop app before Session 1. Covers Module 3.
**Build in:** Screen recording of each surface, produced in PowerPoint or a video editor.

### 2.5 Welcome Packet
**Format:** 1-page PDF.
**Description:** What Camp Claude is, what to bring (laptop, desktop app installed, a real work problem you want to solve), what to expect, and a simple schedule of the five sessions. Sent the week before Session 1.
**Build in:** Word doc, exported to PDF.

### 2.6 Starting-Line Self-Assessment
**Format:** Short web form or 1-page doc.
**Description:** A 2-minute self-check. "Can you explain in your own words what an LLM is?" Routes confident learners past Video 1; everyone else into the full set. Keeps the curriculum respectful of both audiences.
**Build in:** Typeform, Tally, or a 1-page doc with a self-scored rubric.

### 2.7 Tech Setup Guide
**Format:** 1–2 page PDF or web page.
**Description:** Step-by-step checklist for setting up Claude before Session 1. Install the desktop app, log in, verify you can open Cowork, create a "Camp Claude" folder on the desktop. Uses landmark language, not path language.
**Build in:** Word doc or web page.

### 2.8 Calendar Invites
**Format:** ICS file or calendar event links.
**Description:** Pre-built calendar invites for all 5 live sessions, with Zoom or Google Meet links embedded. Sent automatically on enrollment.
**Build in:** Google Calendar, Zoom scheduling, or a tool like Calendly for recurring events.

## 3. Live Cohort Assets

What the facilitator and learners use during the five live sessions.

### 3.1 Facilitator Guide
**Format:** 30–50 page Word doc.
**Description:** The atom of the entire program. Session-by-session script, timing, exercises, prompts, common failure modes, and what to do when a learner gets stuck. Every other live asset derives from this one. Build this first.
**Build in:** Word doc, organized by session with embedded module maps from the syllabus.

### 3.2 Session 1 Deck — Orientation Recap & Chat Fluency
**Format:** PowerPoint, ~15 slides.
**Description:** Quick recap of the pre-work videos, then dives into Modules 4–5 (how to actually talk to Claude, Projects in Chat). Heavy on live exercises where learners work with real tasks.
**Build in:** PowerPoint.

### 3.3 Session 2 Deck — Connectors & Skills
**Format:** PowerPoint, ~15 slides.
**Description:** Covers Modules 6–7. Learners install at least one connector and one skill live during the session. Ends with the "Gmail skill isn't a thing" coda.
**Build in:** PowerPoint.

### 3.4 Session 3 Deck — Graduating to Cowork
**Format:** PowerPoint, ~15 slides.
**Description:** Covers Modules 8–9. The sandbox boundary. The 90-second teaching exercise done as a group, with every learner creating their own "Claude Test" folder on their own desktop.
**Build in:** PowerPoint.

### 3.5 Session 4 Deck — Supervision
**Format:** PowerPoint, ~15 slides.
**Description:** Covers Module 10. Learners give Claude a real multi-file task, review the output, correct what's wrong, and iterate until they feel confident they can catch mistakes.
**Build in:** PowerPoint.

### 3.6 Session 5 Deck — Automation (The Payoff)
**Format:** PowerPoint, ~15 slides.
**Description:** Covers Module 11. The whole cohort builds the same automation together. This is the graduation moment of the entire curriculum — learners end the session with something running in their own account.
**Build in:** PowerPoint.

### 3.7 Session Exercise Handouts
**Format:** Short PDFs or markdown files, one per session.
**Description:** The exercises pulled out of the facilitator guide and given to learners as standalone handouts. Each has the exercise, the graduation moment, and space to take notes.
**Build in:** Word doc exported to PDF, or posted in the community space.

### 3.8 Shared Cohort Project — Morning Inbox Triage
**Format:** Live automation template.
**Description:** The default automation the whole cohort builds together in Session 5. Everyone has email, so it's a universal starting point. Includes a prompt template, connector setup guide, and scheduled task configuration.
**Build in:** Cowork — scheduled task plus Gmail connector plus prompt instructions.

### 3.9 Graduation Checklists
**Format:** 1-page PDF per session.
**Description:** Behavioral checkpoints for each session ("by the end of this session, you can…"). Learners self-report completion, facilitator confirms. This is the assessment model.
**Build in:** Word doc or the community platform.

## 4. Community Assets

The Circle or Skool space that holds the cohort together before, during, and after camp.

### 4.1 Community Space
**Format:** Circle or Skool community.
**Description:** The home base for Camp Claude. Houses all cohorts, alumni, resources, and the video library. Every learner gets an account on enrollment.
**Build in:** Circle or Skool. (Skool is cheaper and simpler; Circle is more customizable — pick based on appetite for setup time.)

### 4.2 Channel Structure
**Format:** Predefined set of channels or spaces inside the community.
**Description:** Default structure — Announcements, Current Cohort, Pre-Work, Automations Built, Wins, Stuck Points, Alumni Lounge, Resources. Every new cohort reuses the same structure.
**Build in:** Configured inside Circle or Skool.

### 4.3 Community Guidelines
**Format:** Single post or page.
**Description:** How to participate, how to ask for help, how to share wins, what's off-topic. Keeps the space productive without being heavy-handed.
**Build in:** Pinned post in the community.

### 4.4 Welcome Post Template
**Format:** Reusable post template.
**Description:** Each new learner introduces themselves with a short template — name, role, one task they want Claude to take off their plate. Creates instant cohort familiarity.
**Build in:** Template post in the community.

### 4.5 Weekly Prompts
**Format:** Set of recurring community posts.
**Description:** Scheduled prompts during and after camp that keep the community alive between sessions. Examples — "what did you delegate to Claude this week?", "show your latest automation," "what stuck point are you hitting?"
**Build in:** Scheduled posts inside Circle/Skool, or a lightweight automation that posts them.

### 4.6 Video Library (Hosted in Community)
**Format:** Organized folder of the four pre-camp videos plus session recordings.
**Description:** The canonical place to rewatch the pre-work videos and catch up on live sessions. Searchable and organized by module.
**Build in:** Video hosting inside Circle/Skool, or embedded Vimeo.

### 4.7 Alumni Lounge
**Format:** Persistent community channel.
**Description:** Where graduates of past cohorts stay. New automations get shared here. New cohorts get welcomed by alumni. This is where the long-term health of the program lives.
**Build in:** Dedicated channel inside the community.

## 5. Post-Camp Assets (Take-Home References)

What graduates reach for after the cohort ends.

### 5.1 The Workbook
**Format:** 40–60 page PDF.
**Description:** Module-by-module reference with the one-sentence rules, exercises, graduation checklists, and landmark language. Not a standalone course — a take-home companion to the cohort. Derived directly from the facilitator guide.
**Build in:** Word doc exported to PDF.

### 5.2 Prompt & Automation Starter Library
**Format:** Folder of short markdown files or a Notion page.
**Description:** Templates for the most common scheduled tasks, connector setups, and Cowork workflows. Lowers the activation energy for learners to build their second, third, and fourth automations on their own.
**Build in:** Folder of markdown files in the community, or a shared Notion page.

### 5.3 Certificate of Completion
**Format:** PDF certificate.
**Description:** Sent to each graduate who completes the program and their capstone automation. A small but meaningful artifact that learners can share on LinkedIn.
**Build in:** Canva template, personalized per graduate.

### 5.4 Testimonial Request Template
**Format:** Email template and form.
**Description:** Sent to graduates a week after they finish. Asks three short questions — what did you build, what changed, would you recommend it. Becomes the source for case studies and marketing.
**Build in:** Email in the post-camp sequence plus a Typeform.

### 5.5 Alumni Newsletter
**Format:** Monthly email.
**Description:** Short update sent to all alumni — new automations to try, new connectors and skills, cohort news, open enrollment announcements. Keeps the relationship warm and drives word-of-mouth.
**Build in:** ConvertKit, Beehiiv, or similar.

## 6. Operations Assets

The behind-the-scenes infrastructure that keeps cohorts running.

### 6.1 Enrollment Tracker
**Format:** Spreadsheet.
**Description:** Master list of enrollees per cohort — name, email, role, payment status, intake answers, progress through the program. One row per learner.
**Build in:** Google Sheets or Airtable.

### 6.2 Session Recording Workflow
**Format:** Documented process.
**Description:** How each live session gets recorded, edited, uploaded to the community, and linked in the cohort channel. Consistency matters — learners need to know where to find recordings.
**Build in:** Zoom or Google Meet recording plus a short editing checklist plus community upload steps.

### 6.3 Payment & Invoicing Setup
**Format:** Stripe or similar integration.
**Description:** How learners pay and how companies get invoiced. Two flows — individual card checkout for open enrollment, invoice-based billing for company cohorts.
**Build in:** Stripe, Stripe Invoicing, or a dedicated tool like Paddle.

### 6.4 Facilitator Prep Checklist
**Format:** 1-page checklist.
**Description:** What to do before every live session — verify the deck, test the Zoom room, confirm the shared project is ready, check the community for stuck points from the previous session. Prevents avoidable session-day chaos.
**Build in:** Single-page Word doc or Notion checklist.

### 6.5 Post-Session Debrief Template
**Format:** Short form or doc.
**Description:** Filled out after each session by the facilitator. What went well, what got stuck, what needs to change for next cohort. The source material for iterating the curriculum.
**Build in:** Notion template or a shared Google Doc.

## 7. Scaling Assets (Optional / Later)

What gets built only after the program has run 5–10 successful cohorts and the format is stable.

### 7.1 Facilitator Training Program
**Format:** Short course plus certification.
**Description:** For people who want to run Camp Claude inside their own company or as a licensed facilitator. Condensed version of the facilitator guide plus a train-the-trainer session plus ongoing support.
**Build in:** Derived from the facilitator guide, delivered as a mini cohort of its own.

### 7.2 Company Cohort Customization Kit
**Format:** Template set.
**Description:** For private company cohorts. Lets the facilitator quickly customize the curriculum to the company's specific tools, workflows, and automations. Makes each company engagement feel bespoke without rebuilding from scratch.
**Build in:** A copy of the facilitator guide plus a customization worksheet.

### 7.3 Code Appendix — Where to Go Next
**Format:** 1–2 page Word doc.
**Description:** The Module 12 signpost. What Code is, who it's for, where to go next if the learner wants to go deeper. Published as an appendix, not a module.
**Build in:** Word doc.

### 7.4 Advanced Automation Track
**Format:** Optional follow-up cohort.
**Description:** For graduates who want to go further. Builds on Camp Claude with more complex multi-step automations, connector chaining, and custom skills. Sold as a standalone follow-up at a higher price point.
**Build in:** A separate curriculum, built only after demand is demonstrated from the first few cohorts.

## Build Order

Build in this order. Each asset informs the next, so working out of order means redoing things.

1. **Facilitator Guide** — the atom. Forces you to actually teach the curriculum and find what breaks.
2. **Session Decks (1–5)** — derived from the facilitator guide.
3. **Pre-Camp Videos (1–4)** — record once, reuse every cohort.
4. **Welcome Packet, Self-Assessment, Tech Setup Guide** — small but required before first cohort.
5. **Community Space** — Circle or Skool, with channel structure and welcome post.
6. **Landing Page + Sales Page + Enrollment Form** — enough to sell the first cohort.
7. **Payment Setup + Enrollment Tracker** — operational minimum.
8. **Run the first cohort** — treat it as playtest and take detailed debrief notes.
9. **Workbook** — derived from the facilitator guide plus cohort notes.
10. **Case Studies + Testimonials** — collected from the first cohort and used to sell the second.
11. **Email Sequences + Social Media Kit** — improve marketing for cohort two onward.
12. **Alumni Newsletter + Starter Library** — introduced by cohort three.
13. **Facilitator Training + Customization Kit** — only after the format is locked, probably after cohort five or later.

The first cohort is the real validation step. Resist over-building before running it. The facilitator guide, five session decks, four videos, a welcome packet, a community space, and a way to take payment are enough to start. Everything else can be built in response to what the first cohort teaches you.
